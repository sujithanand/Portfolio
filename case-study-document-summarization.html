Policy Document Summaries

Phase 1: Problem Definition & Opportunity

Context

Legal and compliance teams routinely review lengthy, dense policy documents to spot changes, assess impact, and trigger actions. Parallel updates increased effort and risk of delay.

How I Identified the Problem

  • Observed users working inside the policy platform, watching how they navigated documents.
  • Interviews with legal and compliance teams about review workflows and delays.
  • Noted repeated manual scanning to extract key points and implications.
  • Mapped where comprehension—not access—was the bottleneck.

Key Problems Identified

  • Reviews were time-consuming; key changes had to be found manually.
  • Critical updates could be missed or acted on late.
  • No structured way to surface follow-ups or owners.
  • Review quality and speed varied across users.

Why This Needed to Be Solved

Slow decision-making increased risk, raised cognitive load, and hurt efficiency when multiple updates landed together. The shift needed was from reading documents to understanding and acting on them.

Phase 2: Solution Design

Strategy

Augment documents with intelligence so users can understand key updates quickly, know what matters, and act confidently—without leaving their workflow.

Solution Overview

Designed an AI-powered briefing system embedded in the policy platform that generates concise summaries, highlights critical changes, and surfaces follow-up actions.

Key Design Interventions

1. AI-Generated Summaries

  • Extract key information from long-form policies into structured, readable briefs.
  • Cut the time to grasp updates.

2. Highlighting Critical Changes

  • Flag important updates and point to the relevant sections.
  • Reduce risk of missing critical information.

3. Action-Oriented Briefings

  • Turn summaries into actionable insights with suggested follow-ups.
  • Shift from passive reading to active decision-making.

4. Embedded Experience

  • Keep briefs inside the policy workflow to avoid context switching.
  • Access insights at the moment of review.

5. AI as Decision Support

  • Position AI as support, not replacement for legal judgment.
  • Keep outputs explainable and trustworthy.

Stakeholder Alignment

Partnered with Product and Legal to balance automation with compliance, iterating outputs to fit real-world legal workflows.

Phase 3: Implementation & Adoption

Implementation

  • Integrated summarization into the platform with structured briefing layouts.
  • Validated outputs through user feedback and iteration.
  • Aligned summaries with legal expectations and standards.

Driving Adoption

  • Made briefs visible in-line to encourage natural use.
  • Reduced effort to process documents, building trust via consistent outputs.
  • Framed as a time-saving enhancement, not a requirement.

Impact

  • Reduced time to review policy documents.
  • Faster understanding and decision-making.
  • Higher confidence in catching critical updates.
  • More consistent review processes across teams.

Reflection

In legal and compliance work, the challenge is sensemaking, not access. Embedding AI in the workflow reduced cognitive load, improved clarity, and accelerated confident decisions—while keeping human judgment in control.

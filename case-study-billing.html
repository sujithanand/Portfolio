Law Firm Billing Acceleration

Reducing billing cycle time from 20 days to 5 by unifying onboarding, rate approvals, and billing into a single guided workflow.

Phase 1: Problem Definition & Opportunity

Context

Legal billing workflows were fragmented across multiple systems; onboarding, rate approvals, and billing ran independently with separate inputs, reviews, and approvals. The fragmentation created delays, redundant work, and low visibility for finance and legal ops.

How I Identified the Problem

  • Process mapping sessions across the full billing lifecycle.
  • Cross-functional workshops with timekeepers, billing managers, and finance.
  • Observed system-hopping and repeated handoffs in real workflows.
  • Time-loss analysis across onboarding, approvals, and billing stages.

The issue was a chain of disconnected steps, not a single bottleneck.

Key Problems Identified

  • Onboarding, approvals, and billing lived in separate systems with no continuity.
  • Multiple handoffs caused redundant reviews and delays.
  • Finance lacked real-time visibility into progress.
  • Manual coordination (emails, follow-ups) was required to move work forward.
  • Errors and missing data surfaced late, causing rework.

Why This Needed to Be Solved

Billing delays hurt cash flow, increased manual workload, and eroded confidence in timelines. Teams spent more time coordinating than completing work. The opportunity: shift from a fragmented, multi-step process to a unified, guided workflow.

Phase 2: Solution Design

Detailed screens are confidential; I’m happy to walk you through the design live. Drop a note at sujithanand96@gmail.com.

Strategy

Design a single, continuous workflow that removes handoffs, reduces duplication, provides cross-role visibility, and embeds validation early.

Solution Overview

A guided billing workflow combining timekeeper onboarding, rate documentation/approvals, and billing execution into one connected experience.

Key Design Interventions

1. Unified Workflow Experience

  • Consolidated steps into one guided flow from onboarding → approvals → billing.
  • Eliminated system switching and reduced context shifts.

2. Persona-Based Workflow Cards

  • Connected cards for Timekeepers, Reviewers, and Finance teams.
  • Each card surfaces relevant actions, dependencies, and ownership.

3. Embedded Compliance & Validation

  • Compliance guardrails and AI-assisted validation early in the flow.
  • Prevents bad data from reaching approval stages.

4. Approval Integration into Billing Queue

  • Approvals live inside the billing workflow, removing duplicate reviews.
  • Shorter wait times between steps.

5. Finance Visibility Dashboard

  • Dashboards to monitor progress, bottlenecks, and pending approvals.
  • Removes reliance on email follow-ups.

Stakeholder Alignment

Workshops aligned timekeepers (ease), legal ops (compliance), and finance (visibility). Demonstrated value via simplified flows and reduced delays.

Phase 3: Implementation & Adoption

Implementation

  • Rolled out the unified workflow incrementally to limit disruption.
  • Validated with prototypes and stakeholder feedback.
  • Integrated with existing systems and processes.

Driving Adoption

  • Made the unified flow easier than legacy methods.
  • Delivered real-time visibility to finance, building trust.
  • Reinforced with alignment sessions and feedback loops.

Impact

  • Billing cycle reduced from 20 days to 5.
  • Redundant approvals and manual coordination eliminated.
  • Improved cash flow and revenue realization.
  • Lower operational load; proactive exception handling.

Reflection

Workflow pain came from system fragmentation, not user behavior. Connecting steps, reducing handoffs, and giving visibility delivered the biggest gains. Designing for the whole workflow, not single screens, drove speed and efficiency.

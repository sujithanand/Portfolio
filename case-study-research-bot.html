Case Study

BotDojo: AI-Powered Research Agent

Unifying customer and product insights that were trapped in Gong, Salesforce, Zendesk, and Productboard into a single, queryable surface so teams could respond faster with confidence.

RoleResearch systems lead

Phase 1: Problem Definition & Opportunity

Context

Insights lived across Gong conversation notes, Salesforce CRM data, Zendesk tickets, and Productboard feedback, yet no single pathway made those signals usable. Teams worked with stale, fragmented information.

How I Identified the Problem

  • Observed research and product workflows that relied on manual synthesis.
  • Stakeholder discussions noted repeated delays in getting signal clarity.
  • Decision patterns relied on partial data or outdated assumptions.
  • Direct research showed insight gathering was slow and inconsistent.

Key Problems

  • Insights were fragmented across tools, requiring manual effort to compile.
  • Research synthesis was time-intensive and not scalable.
  • Teams lacked real-time access to user signals.
  • Decisions were often based on incomplete or outdated data.

Why This Needed to Be Solved

Delays hurt research and design cycles, increased reliance on individuals, reduced confidence, and blocked scaling research across products. The opportunity was to shift from periodic research to continuous, accessible insight.

Phase 2: Solution Design

Strategy

Built a centralized, AI-powered system that aggregates multiple sources, enables natural-language querying, and scales research accessibility.

Solution Overview

Designed and launched BotDojo, an AI research agent that normalizes data, surfaces summaries, and links back to source artifacts.

Core System Design

  • Data inputs: Gong, Salesforce, Zendesk, Productboard.
  • AI layer: aggregation, processing, and synthesis of signals.
  • Interface: queryable surface with contextual summaries.

Key Design Decisions

  • Centralized fragmented data without replacing existing tools.
  • Enabled natural querying to reduce manual filtering.
  • Prioritized fast, usable insights over perfect analysis.
  • Designed for cross-functional use—design, PM, and customer teams.

Stakeholder Alignment

Positioned the system as a way to reduce friction, showed faster insight retrieval, and secured engineering agreement on connector feasibility.

Phase 3: Implementation & Adoption

Implementation

  • Built BotDojo with connectors to the four enterprise tools.
  • Designed a simple interface for querying insights and reviewing sources.
  • Ensured outputs were actionable and surfaced clear next steps.

Driving Adoption

  • Embedded BotDojo into existing workflows rather than building new rituals.
  • Demonstrated use cases inside design and product scenarios.
  • Enabled teams with self-serve access and reduced dependency on centralized researchers.

The principle was to make the system easier to use than not to use.

Impact

  • 50% reduction in research cycle time (3 weeks → 1.5 weeks).
  • Real-time insight access across PM, design, and customer support teams.
  • Less dependency on manual synthesis and faster decision making.
  • Scalable research availability across multiple product teams.

Reflection

The biggest challenge was not the lack of data but the lack of accessibility and synthesis; treating research as a system allowed broader adoption. AI enabled the shift, but the real value was in embedding it meaningfully into workflows.
